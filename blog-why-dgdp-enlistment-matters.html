Why DGDP Enlistment Matters: What It Means When a Supplier Is Verified

January 19, 2026 3 min read

Enlistment Is a Vetting Process, Not a Formality

DGDP enlistment isn't simply a registration step — it's a vetting process covering financial standing, technical capability, and compliance history. A supplier holding active enlistment across a given licence category has been through this review and is legally authorized to represent manufacturers in tenders under that category.

What This Means for the International Manufacturers We Work With

For a manufacturer choosing a Bangladesh-side partner, working with an enlisted supplier is not optional — DGDP tenders can only accept bids from enlisted entities. But beyond the legal requirement, enlistment status is also a useful signal of institutional trust: DGDP and the client institutions (BOF, COD, BAF, NSSD, EDCL) have an established working relationship with the supplier, which tends to translate into smoother tender processing and clearer communication during evaluation.

Breadth of Enlistment Matters Too

Not all enlisted suppliers hold the same category coverage. A supplier enlisted across all 12 DGDP licence categories, as Al-Amin Traders is, can represent a manufacturer's full product range under one relationship, rather than requiring separate partners for different product lines — a meaningful simplification for manufacturers with diverse portfolios.

What to Ask a Potential Partner

Before committing to a local partner, it's reasonable for an international manufacturer to ask directly which licence categories they hold, how long they've held them, and which institutions they have active supply relationships with. These are verifiable facts, not marketing claims, and any established enlisted supplier should be able to answer them clearly.
