What is DGDP and why does enlistment matter?
DGDP Enlistment

The Directorate General of Defence Purchase (DGDP) is the procurement authority under Bangladesh's Ministry of Defence. Only DGDP enlisted suppliers are legally permitted to participate in defence procurement tenders and supply military institutions in Bangladesh. Al-Amin Traders holds enlistment under Licence Categories 1 to 12 — the broadest private sector enlistment available — which authorizes us to procure and supply across all major product categories from military ordnance to pharmaceuticals and mega projects.

Which institutions does Al-Amin Traders supply?
Client Institutions

Al-Amin Traders has active supply relationships with five key government and defence institutions: the Bangladesh Ordnance Factory (BOF) for ammunition and ordnance production support, the Central Ordnance Depot (COD) for military general supplies, the Bangladesh Air Force (BAF) for air force equipment and electronics, the Naval Store Sub Depot (NSSD) for Bangladesh Navy vessel maintenance and marine supplies, and the Essential Drugs Company Limited (EDCL) for pharmaceutical raw materials and laboratory supplies.

How does the DGDP procurement process work?
Procurement Process

Bangladesh's defence procurement follows a formal government tender process managed by DGDP. When an institution like BOF or NSSD requires supplies, DGDP issues a tender notice. Enlisted suppliers submit quotations with technical specifications, pricing and delivery terms. Awarded contracts require delivery to specification, inspection and acceptance before payment is released. Al-Amin Traders manages the full procurement cycle on behalf of international suppliers — from tender response and price negotiation through to delivery, inspection support and documentation.

What military and ordnance products can you supply?
Category 1 — Military & Ordnance

Our Category 1 supply covers: ammunition support materials including propellant chemicals and primer and ignition components; military workshop and maintenance consumables; vehicle spare parts for armored and military vehicles; field and camp equipment including tents, stretchers and camouflage materials; military clothing, boots, helmets and NBC protective suits; military electronics, communication sets, night vision components and optical sighting systems; and explosive ordnance disposal support materials.

What naval and marine products do you supply to NSSD?
Category 2 — Naval / NSSD

Our Category 2 supply to the Naval Store Sub Depot covers seven sub-categories: marine engineering components including propulsion and pump spare parts; deck equipment and hardware; marine electrical and electronics including navigation instruments, radar components and sonar accessories; marine coatings and anti-fouling paints; ship safety and survival equipment including life rafts and fire fighting systems; naval workshop tools and consumables; and vessel maintenance materials including sacrificial anodes, underwater epoxy and bilge pumps.

What pharmaceutical items do you supply to EDCL?
Category 3 — EDCL / Pharmaceutical

Our Category 3 pharmaceutical supply covers Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs) including antibiotics, analgesics, antihypertensives and antidiabetics; pharmaceutical excipients including binders, fillers, disintegrants and coating agents; primary and secondary packaging materials; pharmaceutical laboratory and quality control equipment including HPLC systems and dissolution testers; manufacturing consumables such as filters and gaskets; and medical devices and hospital supplies. All API sources hold WHO-GMP or equivalent certification.

Can you facilitate large-scale defence and industrial projects?
Category 4 — Mega Projects

Yes. Our Category 4 Mega Projects service connects international project companies with Bangladesh's defence and government infrastructure requirements. We facilitate defence infrastructure construction, industrial plant installation, water treatment plant projects, power and energy systems including solar and diesel generator installation, technology and communications infrastructure, and foreign investment and joint venture arrangements with Bangladesh's government and defence sector. Al-Amin Traders acts as the local partner that opens procurement access and manages the institutional engagement.

How can a foreign manufacturer work with Al-Amin Traders?
International Partnership

Foreign manufacturers, exporters and project companies work with Al-Amin Traders as the Bangladesh-side DGDP enlisted agent. The arrangement is straightforward: the foreign company provides product specifications, pricing and supply capacity; Al-Amin Traders handles all procurement engagement with DGDP and the client institutions, submits tenders, manages the local compliance and documentation requirements, and coordinates delivery and inspection. We also advise on which product categories align with current procurement demand and how to position your product for competitive success in Bangladesh's defence market.
